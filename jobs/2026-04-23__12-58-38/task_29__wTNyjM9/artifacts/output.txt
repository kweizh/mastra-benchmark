Ah, the classic omelette! A simple yet elegant dish that can be elevated with just a few techniques. Here’s how to make a delicious French omelette:

### Ingredients:
- 3 large eggs
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1-2 tablespoons of unsalted butter (more butter is always better!)
- Fresh herbs (like chives or parsley), optional
- Cheese (like Gruyère or goat cheese), optional
- Fillings of your choice (mushrooms, bell peppers, ham, etc.), optional

### Instructions:

1. **Prepare the Ingredients**: If you’re using any fillings, chop them finely and sauté them in a little butter until cooked. Set aside.

2. **Whisk the Eggs**: In a bowl, crack the eggs and add a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk them vigorously until the yolks and whites are fully combined and slightly frothy.

3. **Heat the Pan**: Place a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add 1-2 tablespoons of butter. Let it melt and foam, but don’t let it brown.

4. **Cook the Eggs**: Pour the whisked eggs into the pan. Using a spatula, gently stir the eggs in a circular motion, pulling the cooked edges toward the center while tilting the pan to let the uncooked eggs flow to the edges.

5. **Add Fillings**: When the eggs are mostly set but still slightly runny on top, add your desired fillings and a sprinkle of fresh herbs or cheese.

6. **Fold the Omelette**: Carefully fold the omelette in half using your spatula. Let it cook for another 30 seconds to a minute, just until the cheese melts and the inside is cooked to your liking.

7. **Serve**: Slide the omelette onto a plate, and if you like, add a pat of butter on top for extra richness. Garnish with more herbs if desired.

### Tips:
- For a fluffier omelette, you can add a splash of milk or cream to the eggs before whisking.
- Always use fresh, high-quality eggs for the best flavor.
- Experiment with different fillings to find your favorite combination!

Bon appétit! Enjoy your omelette with a bit more butter for that luxurious touch!
