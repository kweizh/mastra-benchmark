Ah, the classic omelette! A simple yet elegant dish that can be elevated with just a few techniques. Here’s how to make a delicious French omelette:

### Ingredients:
- 3 large eggs
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 tablespoon of butter (more if you like it rich!)
- Fresh herbs (like chives or parsley), optional
- Cheese, vegetables, or ham (optional)

### Instructions:

1. **Prepare the Eggs**: Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk them gently until the yolks and whites are fully combined. You want to incorporate some air for a fluffy texture.

2. **Heat the Pan**: Place a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Add the butter and let it melt, swirling it around to coat the pan evenly. The butter should foam but not brown.

3. **Cook the Eggs**: Pour the beaten eggs into the pan. Let them sit for a few seconds until they start to set around the edges. Using a spatula, gently stir the eggs, pushing them from the edges toward the center. This helps create a soft, creamy texture.

4. **Add Fillings (Optional)**: If you’re using cheese, herbs, or other fillings, sprinkle them on one half of the omelette when the eggs are still slightly runny on top.

5. **Fold the Omelette**: Once the eggs are mostly set but still slightly soft, use the spatula to fold the omelette in half over the fillings. Let it cook for another 30 seconds to a minute, just until the inside is cooked to your liking.

6. **Serve**: Slide the omelette onto a plate, and if you like, add a little more butter on top for richness. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

### Tips:
- For a fluffier omelette, you can add a splash of milk or cream to the eggs before whisking.
- Experiment with different fillings to suit your taste!

Bon appétit! Enjoy your omelette with a touch of extra butter for that luxurious finish!
